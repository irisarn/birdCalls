Bird Calls of Oregon - What Bird Do I Hear?

Final Project by Iris Arnold, Jasmin Schaefer, Luisa Zini and Harmony Mosby

Project Background

Throughout the machine learning module of this course we have seen the classification of images to aid in handwriting analysis, identify animals, and to classify flower species with a high degree of accuracy. The utility of the machine learning process can also be seen in many services we use on a day to day basis such as recommendation systems (Netflix and YouTube), search engines (Google and Bing), and social media feeds (Facebook and twitter). For our project we wanted to take a deeper look at a domain in which the application of the machine learning process seems to be relatively scarce, sound classification. The real world application of this type of machine learning platform can aid in areas such as predictive maintenance in industrial settings and aid the deaf community in daily activities. While we did touch on sound classification briefly with the prediction of gender based on acoustic properties of voice and speech, we wanted to take this a step further and see if we could train a machine learning model to be able to classify a sound based off of a raw audio file. To demonstrate the machine learning process on the classification of sound we focused on the identification of bird species based on audio clips of bird calls.
